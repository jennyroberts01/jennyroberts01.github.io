About me

I am a post-doctoral researcher in the Alfred-Wegener Institute in Germany. My research interests are focused on reconstructing Earth's climate over the last deglaciation. I am currently working as part of project (COPTER) that aims to quantify the rates of carbon dioxide outgassing from the South Pacific over the last deglaciation.

I use a range of geochemical proxies (e.g. Δ14C, δ11B, δ18O, δ13C) measured on benthic and planktonic foraminifera to reconstruct changes in the carbonate chemistry of South Pacific. More recently, I have been working on developing the δ7Li proxy as a independent means to reconstruct changes in ocean pH in the past.
